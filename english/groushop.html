This is a course project. Groupshop is a platform to find/create group order for online shopping. The platform provides convenience in finding groups buying together to share/avoid delivery fees, and combines different retail brands so that users don’t need to create different accounts on each brand.



Overview



Problem Statement



Neighborhood context

There are a lot of Chinese students living in Ann Arbor now. As most of the supermarkets are located in the surrounding area of the city, those without a car need to take a bus or uber to the supermarket to buy groceries, which is either time consuming or too expensive in transportation. Some of them will buy a lot of things at one time, but it’s impossible for those who prefer fresh vegetables and fruits or are busy with study. So, those people turn to delivery services provided by different supermarkets to receive everyday orders. As a Result of the convenient delivery service in China, many Chinese students are more used to buying everything online.

1. Chinese students living in Ann Arbor without a car
2. Busy with study
3. Prefer eating fresh vegetables and fruits
4. Be used to delivery service

Current situation



The delivery fee of each supermarket is high or can be avoided by buying certain amounts. So, some of the students may share delivery fees with roommates or friends. But still, it’s hard to find someone to share fees and if there are few people to share, each person needs to buy a large amount of products with a high delivery cost.
Also, if their friends don’t live in the same building, they often need to negotiate the shipping address, which may cause conflicts. They also need to calculate the money to split up, and find a suitable way to transfer money because they possibly don’t have the same bank account. And usually all the products in one order will be packed together. It’s difficult to sort out each person’s products.

1. Share delivery fee with roommates or friends, but still each person needs to buy a lot of things with a still high delivery cost.
2. Sometimes it's hard to find someone to share the delivery fee
3. Buy a lot of things alone
4. Cause conflicts in deciding the shipping address, calculate and transfer money.
5. With one person checked out, all the products are in one package and other people must sort out what they buyed by taking notes on their orders.

Features of App

1. Provide a platform for those people who need to find someone to share delivery fees to shop online.
2. Help those who form a group to share delivery fees a tool to communicate and coordinate issues like where to deliver, how to pay for the bill
3. Help gather all the supermarket brands available for online delivery so that users don’t need to create accounts on different platforms.

Approaches

Design rationale

1. It is a better way to satisfy both safety and better experiences to force users to verify their email and mobile phone, and create a profile. Although it may be more time consuming than only email and password, the requirement to link to the phone provides a safer environment for all the users because the app can ensure every user’s actions can be tracked. And the profile information can provide more humannesses and better recommendations from apps to ensure overall experiences. However, this can also be improved in later steps, so it can be edited later and allowed to skip, leading to a shorter way to finish the registration process.

2. There are two ways to display group order lists, one with the map, the other without map. As Figure 5 shows, these two options perform almost the same. It depends on what users care about. If users really care about their privacy, the list view without a map can also provide a flexible way to help them find a suitable group order using the filter. If users want to search for nearest group orders faster, they can use the map view to locate a group order.

3. Approach 2 has a different user flow as Approach 1. As Figure 6 shows, Approach 2 has a better workflow considering users’ needs. First, it has flexibility for users to decide if they need a group order to share delivery fees or not according to how much they have already spent. If their amounts have reached the amount to waive delivery fee, they don’t need a group. Secondly, users can choose whatever supermarket brand they want before deciding on the group order. They don’t need to rely on one group members’ preferences, so they have more opportunities to buy what they want. But there are still two trade-offs for this approach. One is after choosing the supermarket brand and want to find a group order, there may not be enough options for users. And the other one is that it may cause more time to wait for group members to finish the shopping.

4. The group order list page is the most important page for this feature. It forms a community and platform to connect with people with the same needs. As Figure 7 shows, this design is the best option to present the group. The avatar of the organizer has some sense of humanness and community. And meanwhile, it is simple and clear, compared to avatars showing all the group members, and provides enough useful information like remaining vacants for group helping users to make some decisions.

5. The login order of Approach 3 is different from the other two approaches. According to Figure 8, putting the login process after users have actions in the app can help users know more about the app, and then make decisions. But it may serve a great disturbance in the overall shopping experience because when users select an item in the list, it means that users have already made some decisions. If the login process appears at this time, users may feel quite annoyed.

6. In approach 3, users need to get confirmation when joining the group. As Figure 9 shows, confirmation performs well in ensuring safety and provides a chance for the organizer to know the applicant. But it’s not a fast and convenient way, and it cannot be ensured that someone may deliberately reject applicants which will cause bad experiences. Also, the organizer has the power to move members out of the group, the option without confirmation can kind of ensure safety. And convenience is the most concerning element for this feature.

Feedback

1. Make prepayments before joining the group in order to avoid the failure in finishing the group order.
2. Display the current amount the group has already reached to better inform users and help them make decisions in joining the group.
3. Let the system automatically calculate the current lowest amount needed for the group to avoid delivery cost. If a user didn’t meet that amount, he/she cannot join the group. The group organizer can open/close this setting of whether his/her group needs this limitation.
